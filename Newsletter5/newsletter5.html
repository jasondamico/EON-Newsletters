An Entrepreneur's Guide to Podcast Listening

I always wanted to get into listening to podcasts, but never found the time to do so. The pandemic has presented the perfect opportunity for me to deep dive into the world of podcasts. Whether you‚Äôre back on campus or learning remotely, the abundance of free time that we all still have is the perfect time for listening. It‚Äôs a great activity to relax but still keep your mind active.

The key is to find the right podcast: the perfect blend of relaxation and positive content. Listening to podcasts with an entrepreneurial perspective isn‚Äôt all about listening to business-related podcasts, it‚Äôs about picking up new learning to better yourself and expand your mind. Don‚Äôt know where to start? No need to worry, your friends at EON have culminated a list of podcasts for you to use to wind down and better yourself!

  1. How I Built This with Guy Raz. An absolute essential for podcast listeners who have an entrepreneurial interest. Host Guy Raz interviews entrepreneurs and business owners from a wide range of industries, who provide commentary on the decisions they made to bring their ideas forward into the world. Great listen for those looking for inspiring stories of triumph, innovation and overcoming challenges.
  2. On Purpose with Jay Shetty. This podcast is a change of pace from the others on this list: Shetty hosts conversations surrounding wellness and self-improvement. The honesty and vulnerability of the conversations here lead to incredible growth and introspection.
  3. Code Switch. This podcast from NPR provides incredible commentary on race in our society. Some episodes are imformative highlights of lesser-known racial justice movements (such as the San Francisco State College protests of 1968) and some episodes analyze major acts of discrimination. This podcast will be sure to deepen your knowledge surrounding race-based issues, a key aspect of becoming a better human (and, therefore, a better entrepreneur).
  4. Ologies with Alie Ward. Alie Ward creates the perfect atmosphere for comical, but intellectual discussion surrounding obscure branches of science. A recent reviewer called the podcast an ‚Äúeducational hug,‚Äù which is an excellent description. Ologies is very easy to digest: after each episode, you will feel just as educated as you are entertained. For a charming, fun podcast that will give you more expertise than you thought possible in all sorts of interesting things (take a recent episode on Tazmanian devils, for instance), look no further.
  5. This Week in Tech (TWiT). TWiT is a comprehensive, friendly report on the state of the tech industry. The podcast is more focused on the broad trajectory of technology, its various advancements and what this means for society as we know it. In an increasingly automated and tech-driven world, the good folks at TWiT will help guide you and keep you informed.

Hopefully these podcasts will give you a good starting point. Please, let us know how you like our recommended shows and if you have any suggestions for us ‚Äî we would love to hear what you think!

Remembering Through Art

(Sata Diakite ‚Äò21)

Since the beginning of the Black Lives Matter (BLM) movement in 2013, the powerful and engaging usage of art has been a massive part of the communication between the movement and the general public. Each art piece, ‚Äúprofessionally made‚Äù (whatever that means) or not, is a tangible and immortal reminder of the injustices that People of Color (POC) and Black Individuals have faced in the United States. Here at Union College, there are students who are using the powerful form of activism and entrepreneurship of art to further inform Union campus on the BLM movement and those who deserve justice for their untimely death and or severe mistreatment. TikTok Ban: What's going on?

by Emma Lee

TikTok, the app made famous by Gen Zer‚Äôs viral dance challenges, cute animals, and the likes, is in talks to be acquired by Microsoft- a company not known for any of the above. Some might question what motivation Microsoft has for acquiring the social media entity, which on the ‚Äúsurface‚Äù doesn‚Äôt seem to jive with Microsoft‚Äôs largely business-focused business. This is a unique opportunity for Microsoft to position itself against giants Facebook INC and Snapchat INC in the social media arena. Microsoft had been in talks to acquire TikTok from Chinese company Bytedance for weeks, but it has recently made national headlines as Trump takes a more aggressive policy stance towards China. This bad blood has led to an Executive threat to ban TikTok in the US unless it is bought by a US company within the next month or so. Recently added to the cutting block was Chinese owned WeChat, which if banned would be even more damning.

Legitimate concerns over privacy issues on the app have, in part, spurred this reaction, but it is also fueled by an ongoing disagreement between China and the US (see the closing of the Chinese Consulate in Texas and increasing conflict in the South China Sea for more). The political tension puts Microsoft in a unique position during their negotiation; the company has been constantly informing the White House of any updates in negotiation, which typically does not happen during a negotiation led by a private company.

Trump arguably toed the line of extortion the other day during a press conference when he said "Whether it's Microsoft or somebody else or it was the Chinese. What the price is, the United States could, should get a very large percentage of that price because we're making it possible." With just over a month before negotiations are set to end, it will be interesting to see how they pan out and what it means for the future of Microsoft, one of the largest tech companies in the world, and notably not invited to Congress‚Äô Antitrust hearing on July 29th.
